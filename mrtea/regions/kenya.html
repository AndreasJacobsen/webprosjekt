Introduction:

If you love hot beverages East Africa might be a place you'd love to visit. Here you'll find Kenya, a small country who just started making tea compared with the other tea producers, but who now is the third largest exporter of tea in the world. To top it of they are also a major coffee exporter.

History:

Kenya first started producion tea in 1903 and is thus the oldest tea producing country in Africa, but still young compared to India and China.

While the first seeds were imported in 1903 commercial tea production started in 1924 with suprisingly promising yelds. Due to the climate, vulcanic soil, sunny days and predictable rain tea can be grown cheaply and efficintly in Kenya with minimal use of fertilizers.

Current state:

Today tea production in Kenya is divided in two, on one is the Kenya Tea Development Board making around 60% of Kenyas tea outup the other is the large scale international tea companies.


Kenya Tea Development Agency - KTDA
KTDA manages small tea farms in Kenya. The farmers can buy equitment and ge help from KTDA, when the crop is ready they also sell their crops to KTDA who then distribute it to he global market.
KTDA manages about 65% of the tea production in Kenya, all of that tea is made without the use of pestisides and picked by hand.
Due to Kenyas unique climate much of the tea is also produced without the use of fertilizers.
Large scale producers use more modern machinery with harvesting machines and larger plantations. This tea is not sold by the KTDA but major coorporation often want to expand production in Kenya due to the efficient production there.
